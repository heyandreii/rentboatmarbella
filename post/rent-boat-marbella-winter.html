Renting a boat in Marbella in winter: a great idea

Updated May 2026 · 5 min read

When you picture Marbella you probably imagine summer, but renting a boat in Marbella in winter is one of the best-kept secrets on the Costa del Sol. While the rest of Europe reaches for its coat, here the sun keeps warming the deck, the sea settles into a calm, and the coves that are packed with anchored boats in August are yours alone. If you want a day at sea without the crowds, with golden light and better prices, the low season is arguably the most beautiful time of year to sail.

The Costa del Sol climate in winter

The Costa del Sol is not called the "coast of the sun" by accident. Marbella enjoys more than 300 days of sunshine a year, and winter is no exception: the Sierra Blanca mountains shelter the bay from cold northerly winds and create a mild microclimate that keeps temperatures well above the European average.

In the middle of January the thermometer often reads between 16 and 20 °C around midday, with clear skies and a pleasant warmth the moment the sun is out. And there is one detail that sailors treasure above all else: without the summer sea breeze, the water wakes up on many mornings completely calm, like a mirror. Gliding over flat water, with no swell and no wind, turns any outing into a serene experience that is harder to find in high season.

The advantages of sailing in the low season

Beyond the weather, renting in winter has concrete advantages that lift the experience exactly where it matters.

Fewer crowds and more availability

In summer you have to book weeks ahead and compete for the best dates. In winter the sea is yours: quiet harbours, no queues at the moorings and a diary with room for whichever day you choose. That sense of exclusivity only grows when you are practically the only boat in the bay.

Better prices and flexibility

The lower demand of the low season means it is easier to lock in your date and to enjoy more comfortable conditions. If you have some flexibility, it is the smartest way to enjoy the same premium boat for less. You can check the figures in our guide to how much it costs to rent a boat in Marbella to see exactly what to expect.

Golden light and empty coves

The winter sun sits lower on the horizon and bathes the coastline in warm, golden light for much of the day, not just at sunset. It is a gift for photos and for the mood on board. What is more, the quiet coves between Puerto Banús and Cabopino, which in summer are shared with dozens of boats, fall silent and deserted in winter, leaving them entirely to you.

What to do on board

Winter does not shrink the plan, it changes it for the better. These are the experiences that work best in the low season:

  • Dolphin watching. The Strait and the Alborán Sea are alive all year round, and with less maritime traffic it is easier to enjoy dolphin watching off Marbella in calm conditions.
  • Early sunset. In winter the sun sets around 18:30, so a sunset yacht tour fits perfectly into an afternoon outing without an early start or a late dinner. The golden light of a winter sundown over La Concha mountain is unforgettable.
  • Relaxed cruising. With a calm sea you can enjoy laid-back routes around the bay, dropping anchor in empty coves for a coffee on board or a quick dip for the brave.

Every outing includes a professional multilingual skipper, swimming gear, water and ice, and the freedom to bring your own food and drink with no corkage fee.

Frequently asked questions about winter sailing

Can you rent a boat in Marbella in winter?

Yes, we sail all year round from Puerto Banús. Thanks to the Costa del Sol's 300-plus days of sunshine and mild winters, there are plenty of calm-sea days that are perfect for heading out between October and May.

Is it too cold to sail in the low season?

Less than you might think. Around midday the mild temperatures often reach 16-20 °C. With a light windproof jacket you will be perfectly comfortable, and the low winter sun bathes the deck in a beautiful golden light.

What happens if the sea is rough that day?

If conditions make it unsafe to sail, we reschedule your outing for another date or refund you in full. You will never lose your money to the weather.
