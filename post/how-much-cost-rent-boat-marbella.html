How much does it cost to rent a boat in Marbella?

Updated July 2026 · 6 min read

So, how much does it cost to rent a boat in Marbella? On a private skippered charter, prices run from €1,200 for two hours to €3,000 for the full day. But the number on its own tells you very little: what really matters is what that rate includes, how many guests come aboard and whether the boat is yours alone or shared with strangers. In this guide we give you honest, transparent 2026 prices — no small print — so you know exactly what to expect.

What's included in the price?

One of the reasons a price from one operator isn't comparable to another is what sits inside it. Our rate includes, at no extra cost:

  • A professional skipper on board for the whole trip (multilingual: English, Spanish, French and Russian).
  • Fuel for the usual route around the bay of Marbella and Puerto Banús.
  • Swimming gear: a floating mat, snorkelling equipment and towels.
  • Water and ice on board; you're welcome to bring your own food and drink with no corkage charge.
  • VAT included in the price you see. No surprises at the end.

The only optional extras are the touches you choose yourself: catering, décor for a marriage proposal, a photographer, or a longer route such as the day trip to Gibraltar, where the additional fuel is quoted separately.

An honest comparison: marketplaces vs a private charter

If you search the big boating marketplaces you'll spot listings "from €60 per person" and wonder why we're talking about €1,200. The answer is simple: they aren't the same thing.

The lowest prices almost always mean shared trips — you board a boat with strangers, on a fixed timetable and a set route — or bareboat hire you have to skipper yourself. For a group of eight, "€60 per person" is €480… but you're sharing the deck, the cool box and the music with another group you've never met.

A premium private charter changes the experience completely: the boat is yours, you decide the route and the timing, there's a skipper who knows every cove, and you get the exclusivity of a brand-new designer yacht. We don't compete on price; we compete on making the day perfect.

It's also worth reading the headline figure carefully. A marketplace "from" price is usually the lowest midweek, low-season, shortest-trip rate — and rarely includes fuel, VAT or a skipper. Once you add those back in, the gap narrows quickly, and you're left comparing a genuinely private day with a shared one. For a special occasion, that difference is the whole point.

What pushes the price up or down

Season

July and August are the busiest months, so it's worth booking weeks ahead. From October to May there's far more availability and better rates: if you have any flexibility, an off-season charter is an excellent, more affordable idea, and the light on a calm winter morning is something special.

Duration

The hourly price falls the longer the trip: a full day works out far better per hour than a couple of hours on their own. If you'd like to swim in several coves, the half day is the sweet spot — perfect for a relaxed dolphin-watching loop with time to anchor and swim.

Extras and the occasion

A basic sunset tour fits neatly into two hours; a celebration with catering, décor and a photographer lifts the budget but remains a fraction of what the same event would cost on land.
